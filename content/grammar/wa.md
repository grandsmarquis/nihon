## Meaning

`は` marks the topic: the thing the sentence is about. It is written with the hiragana `は`, but pronounced `wa` when used as a particle.

## Usage

Use `は` after a noun or noun phrase when introducing, contrasting, or returning to a topic.

| Pattern | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Noun + は | As for noun |
| Topic + は + comment | Speaking of topic, comment |

## Notes

`は` does not always identify the grammatical subject. It frames the sentence around a topic, then the rest of the sentence says something about that topic.
