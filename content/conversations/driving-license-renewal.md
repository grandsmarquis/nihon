## Teaching Notes

This conversation uses polite service-counter Japanese. The visitor asks simple questions, and the clerk gives step-by-step instructions.

- `〜てください` appears often because staff members are giving instructions.
- `〜てもいいですか` is used to ask permission.
- `〜なければなりません` explains a required step.
- `〜あとで` connects the order of procedures.
- `ので` gives a reason in a polite, natural way.
