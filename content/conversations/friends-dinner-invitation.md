## Teaching Notes

This conversation is friendlier and more relaxed than the public-office examples. It uses plain forms in many places, but the main invitation pattern `〜ませんか` appears because it is a natural, gentle way to invite someone.

- `〜ませんか` makes the dinner invitation sound polite and easy to accept or decline.
- `〜ましょう` is used once the friends agree on a plan.
- `から` gives reasons, such as being tired or it being Friday.
- `〜たり〜たり` lists possible after-dinner activities.
- Existing plan and desire grammar appears naturally through `〜たい`, `予定`, and `〜つもり`.
